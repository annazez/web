title: 'Clinically-Grounded Personalisation Engine'
lang: en
context: 'Generic wellness apps offer generic advice. This application needs to deliver contextually appropriate content to users in genuinely varied psychological states - from mild stress management to active mood episodes. A one-size response is actively harmful in this domain.'
decision: 'The personalisation engine is seeded by three validated clinical instruments: WHO-5 (general wellbeing), GAD-7 (anxiety severity), and PHQ-9 (depression screening). These questionnaires are administered periodically and at contextually appropriate moments (not as intrusive popups). The resulting scores form a dynamic user state model that governs content recommendations, exercise difficulty, diary prompt tone, and notification frequency. AI-driven recommendation adjustments are made transparently, with users able to inspect why a particular recommendation was surfaced. The engine does not diagnose; it contextualises. Clinical interpretation remains the domain of qualified professionals within the integration layer.'
consequences: 'More complex content structure and recommendation logic than a simple rule-based system. Requires an ongoing clinical advisory relationship to validate scoring thresholds and response protocols. The tradeoff is a system that provides genuinely personalised support rather than performing personalisation as a marketing feature.'
